Strange lights were being seen in Munroe Falls, Ohio, U.S.A., back in 1967. For at least a week, the Goodyear UFO Society was receiving reports almost nightly of "orange-colored lights of various shapes and sizes."

During a storm on March 30, David Morris was driving around 2:30am when he saw one of the orange lights coming from a few yards to the left side of the road. Morris slowed down to take a look. It was a cone-shaped object around 25 feet high and 12 feet diameter.

Morris caught something in front of the car that made his head face the highway again. He looked just in time to see 4 or 5 figures dressed in strange, orange-coloured uniforms. He hit the brakes but was unable to keep from hitting one of them. The car slid a few feet and he came to a halt. He was about to get out to help when a sudden thought came to his head, "If I killed one of them they'll kill me!" He thrust his foot back on the accelerator and sped away.

Everything indicated that David Morris was telling the truth. After the extra-terrestrial hit-and-run the lights mysteriously ceased to be seen. Some time later, red lights were being reported rather than orange, but no reports of any more roadkill surfaced.
